Overview

Meghalaya — the "abode of clouds" — features rolling hills, living root bridges, and rich Khasi and Garo cultural traditions. It's prized for its monsoon landscapes and hill-town experiences.

Top Attractions

Living Root Bridges (Cherrapunji/Mawsynram areas)

Natural bridges grown from living rubber tree roots over generations, some double-decked, offering unique examples of bio-engineering in the rainforest valleys.

Shillong and its lakes

Capital city known as the "Scotland of the East," featuring colonial architecture, Ward's Lake, Umiam Lake, and a vibrant music scene blending traditional and modern styles.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October to May for clearer skies and hill walks.
  • Transport: Roads can be wet and slippery in monsoon; hire local drivers who know the terrain.
  • Local etiquette: Respect tribal customs and ask before entering private community spaces.

Economy & Development

Meghalaya's economy is based on agriculture, horticulture, and tourism. The state is known for its organic farming, floriculture, and bamboo products. Tourism and eco-tourism are growing sectors, with infrastructure development focused on connectivity and urban amenities.

Industrial Growth

Key sectors include agriculture, handloom, and food processing. The state is investing in IT parks and eco-tourism.

Infrastructure Development

Recent projects include new highways, airport expansion, and smart city initiatives in Shillong. The state is focusing on sustainable development and disaster resilience.

Modern Trends & Lifestyle

Meghalaya is embracing eco-tourism, digital literacy, and youth entrepreneurship. Urban centers are seeing growth in education and technology.

Food Culture

Local cuisine features rice, fish, bamboo shoots, and herbal stews. Modern cafes and fusion restaurants are emerging in towns.

Art & Entertainment

The state has a vibrant tradition of music, dance, and festivals. Art exhibitions and cultural events are gaining popularity.

Notable Personalities

Seasonal Highlights

Best Time to Visit

October to April is ideal for pleasant weather and festivals. Monsoon (June-September) brings lush greenery and vibrant landscapes.

Seasonal Events

  • Winter: Shillong Autumn Festival, Christmas
  • Spring: Shad Suk Mynsiem, Wangala Festival
  • Monsoon: Behdienkhlam, green countryside
  • Autumn: Nongkrem Dance, Diwali
